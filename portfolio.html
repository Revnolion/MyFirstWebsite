Darkness of Anima

Darkness of Anima was the final project I worked on as part of my studies at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment in Seattle, WA. This project is a turn-based RPG and was the culmination of 4 months of hard work with 9 people in the Unity game engine. I served as director and as one of the 3 game designers on the project. Some of my responsibilites included working on level design layouts, building the environments in-engine as we got assets from the artists, and I directed the team during production, taking suggestions and feedback from fellow peers in development to further improve the game. We got the game to a finished prototype state and released it at the end of the school year publicly on the indie game service itch.io.

Living Nightmare

Living Nightmare was the final project of my first year at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment in Seattle, WA. This project is a puzzle-horror game and was the result of 2 months of work with 9 people in the Unity engine. I served as the lead game designer on the project. Some of my responsibilites included working on level design layouts, building environments, and assisting my designer peers throughout production. We got the game to a finished prototype state and released it at the end of that school year publicly on the indie game server itch.io.
