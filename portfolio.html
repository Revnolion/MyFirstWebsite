Particle Paint

Project Release: TBA

Particle Paint is a small project I've been working on in my free time in Unreal Engine 5. It is a small painting game/software that utilizes Unreal Engine's particle systems to allow you to draw/paint using particle effects. I have currently developed systems that allows multiple brushes, brush sensitivity (so the particles are bigger depending on mouse stroke), export to a PNG image, and more. I currently have multiple major updates in the works as well. This project will be released to the public in the near future, however I have not decided yet whether I'm going to charge for it or not. Darkness of Anima

Project Release: July 2022

Darkness of Anima was the final project I worked on as part of my studies at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment in Seattle, WA. This project is a turn-based RPG and was the culmination of 4 months of hard work with 9 people in the Unity game engine. I served as director and as one of the 3 game designers on the project. Some of my responsibilites included working on level design layouts, building the environments in-engine as we got assets from the artists, and I directed the team during production, taking suggestions and feedback from fellow peers in development to further improve the game. We got the game to a finished prototype state and released it at the end of the school year publicly on the indie game service itch.io.

Living Nightmare

Project Release: July 2021

Living Nightmare was the final project of my first year at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment in Seattle, WA. This project is a puzzle-horror game and was the result of 2 months of work with 9 people in the Unity engine. I served as the lead game designer on the project. Some of my responsibilites included working on level design layouts, building environments, and assisting my designer peers throughout production.
