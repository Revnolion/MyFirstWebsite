About Me!

The Core of My Drive

I was born in 2001 in Bremerton, WA. My first computer, an old Windows XP laptop, sparked a lifelong curiosity for technology and deep technical tinkering.

That curiosity led to my first custom-built desktop (GTX 960 and i7-930). I've since upgraded that machine many times over, eventually building the high-end desktop I use today. This decade-long process taught me valuable lessons in systems architecture and relentless problem-solving.

This hands-on, persistent approach to hardware directly translates to my work in software development: I enjoy building robust, efficient systems from the ground up.

Real-World Discipline

Since Late Spring 2024, I've worked as a provision loader for the Puget Sound Naval Shipyard. Loading food onto naval vessels is a demanding job focused on logistics and reliability.

Though non-technical, this experience has been invaluable in forging core professional traits: unwavering reliability, meticulous planning, and the ability to perform under high pressure. These skills ensure I maintain clean version control, adhere to production schedules, and function as a dependable member of any development team.

I also co-founded an indie game studio with a fellow alum from my last school. While our projects are still in the planning stages, this venture is developing my skills in project scoping and collaborative direction.

My Expertise & Future Vision

I gained my formal foundation at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment in Seattle, WA (2020-2022), focusing on Game Design and Production.

My primary expertise is in systems and gameplay programming using Godot, GDScript, and Unreal Engine 5 (Blueprints). I am passionate about creating reusable, efficient engine tools and solving complex gameplay logic challenges.

Game development is my life-long passion. I am actively seeking a role as a Gameplay Programmer, Game Designer or Tools Developer where I can combine my technical skills and disciplined approach to build engaging experiences.
